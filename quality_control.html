Section 1

Commitment to Precision

At Shreeji Construction, quality is not an audit function — it is a foundational engineering discipline embedded at every stage of our project lifecycle. We are unequivocally committed to delivering structural and fabrication work that conforms to the technical specifications, dimensional tolerances, and material standards agreed upon with our industrial partners.

Our Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Control (QC) framework applies to all project scopes — from raw material procurement and structural fabrication through to erection, alignment, and final handover. No component leaves our fabrication bay without satisfying the applicable acceptance criteria.

  • check_circle Compliance with IS 2062, IS 800 (structural steel), and applicable AWS D1.1 welding codes.
  • check_circle Material test certificates (MTCs) verified and retained for every steel consignment received on-site.
  • check_circle Senior QC Engineers assigned to each project for independent oversight and approval authority.
  • check_circle Client and third-party inspector access facilitated at all stages of fabrication and erection.

Non-Conformance Management

Any deviation from specified requirements is documented in a formal Non-Conformance Report (NCR). Each NCR is reviewed by the QC Engineer, dispositioned with a corrective action plan, and closed only after verified rework acceptance.

Section 4

Continuous Improvement

Shreeji Construction operates under a culture of structured, evidence-based improvement. Quality performance data gathered across all concurrent projects is systematically reviewed to identify trends, root causes, and opportunities to raise our engineering standards project after project.

Our improvement process follows a structured Plan–Do–Check–Act (PDCA) cycle, applied at the project level and reinforced by periodic management reviews.

Plan
Pre-Project Quality Planning

A project-specific Quality Plan and ITP is issued for every contract. Lessons learned from prior projects are formally embedded into procedures before works commence.

Do
Execution with Built-In Quality Gates

Inspection hold points, witness points, and daily QC supervisor rounds ensure quality is verified in real time during fabrication and erection — not retrospectively.

Check
Performance Monitoring & NCR Analysis

NCR frequency, weld rejection rates, and rework hours are tracked per project. Monthly QC performance reports are prepared and reviewed by senior management and shared with clients on request.

Act
Corrective & Preventive Actions

Root cause analysis is conducted for all significant non-conformances. Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPAs) are formally documented, assigned, and verified for effectiveness before closure.

Welder Skill Enhancement Programme: All welders undergo periodic re-qualification and guided upskilling to maintain and advance their certification grades, directly reducing weld rejection rates across our projects.
