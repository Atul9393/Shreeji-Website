Section 1

Commitment to Safety

Shreeji Construction is unequivocally committed to protecting the health, safety, and welfare of every worker, contractor, visitor, and stakeholder present on our project sites. The prevention of workplace injuries and occupational illnesses is a core business objective — not an afterthought.

Our overarching goal is the execution of every project — be it structural fabrication, erection, or maintenance shutdown — with zero Lost Time Incidents (LTI). This goal is achieved through a proactive safety culture that places individual responsibility and collective vigilance at its centre.

  • check_circle Full compliance with the Factories Act, 1948 and the Building and Other Construction Workers (BOCW) Act, 1996.
  • check_circle Adherence to OSHA-aligned and IS-standard safe work practices across all disciplines.
  • check_circle Senior management commitment — safety performance is reviewed at every project milestone.

Section 4

Safety Training

A well-trained workforce is our primary line of defence. Shreeji Construction mandates structured safety education for all personnel, from new recruit induction through to advanced skill-specific certifications.

01
New Hire Safety Orientation

Mandatory induction for every new employee or sub-contractor before site access is granted. Covers site layout, evacuation routes, fire safety, and basic hazard awareness.

02
Skill-Specific Safety Training

Targeted modules for welders, fitters, riggers, and supervisors. Includes hot-work safety, rigging load calculations, confined space entry, and scaffolding inspection.

03
First Aid & Emergency Response

Certified first aider(s) present on every active site. Periodic mock drills for fire, medical emergencies, and spill response are conducted across all concurrent sites.

04
Periodic Refresher & Audit Training

Quarterly safety refreshers for all site personnel. Annual external EHS audits validate compliance and identify improvement areas aligned with IS/OSHA benchmarks.
